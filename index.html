The National Cable and Telecommunications Association, or NCTA ("NICK-ta") represents the largest cable companies and ISPs, and it's one of the most powerful lobbies in Washington.

They targeted these ads at your lawmakers, blanketing The Hill and the Washington Post. The message–that Title II would increase taxes & fees–was cleverly aimed at the new Republican majority. And it's completely false. The 1998 Internet Tax Freedom Act prohibits new taxes on Internet access, regardless of what the FCC decides on net neutrality.

Net neutrality keeps our bills down. Without it, cable companies' power to charge websites for special access will drive up the cost of everything we do online, from shopping to Netflix. And if you think Comcast is going to use those extra profits to lower your bill, you're crazy.
